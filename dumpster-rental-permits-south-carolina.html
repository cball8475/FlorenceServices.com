Do You Need a Permit for a Dumpster in South Carolina?

Permit rules for Florence, Darlington, Marion, and other Pee Dee cities — when you need one and when you don't.

Home › Blog › Dumpster Permit Guide SC

Published March 20, 2026 · By Florence SC Services

One of the most common questions we hear from Florence County homeowners and contractors: "Do I need a permit to put a dumpster in my driveway?" The short answer for most Pee Dee region residents is no — but there are situations where you do need one, and getting it wrong can mean fines or having your container removed.

Here's the complete breakdown of dumpster permit rules for Florence SC and surrounding communities.

The Simple Rule

💡 Easy rule of thumb: If the dumpster fits on your private property (driveway, yard, parking lot), you almost never need a permit in South Carolina. If it has to go on a public street, sidewalk, or right-of-way, you need to check with your local municipality.

City-by-City Permit Rules

Florence, SC

The City of Florence does not require a permit for dumpsters placed on private property. If your container must be placed on a public street or within the city right-of-way, you'll need approval from the City of Florence Public Works department. Darlington, SC

Darlington follows a similar rule — no permit for private property placement. However, the City of Darlington requires building permits for any construction, repair, or demolition work exceeding $200 within city limits. The building permit covers the construction activity, not the dumpster itself, but it's worth knowing if your project triggers this requirement. Contact Darlington Codes Enforcement at (843) 398-4029 for details. See our Darlington SC dumpster rental page for local pricing and provider information.

Marion, SC

Marion County generally does not require permits for dumpsters on private property. For placement on public roads or rights-of-way in the City of Marion, contact the Marion city offices. For county roads outside city limits, check with Marion County directly. Your dumpster provider can usually advise on local requirements and handle the permit process if needed.

Other Pee Dee Cities

Hartsville, Lake City, Dillon, and Mullins all follow the same general pattern: no permit needed on private property, permit may be required for street placement. When in doubt, your local dumpster provider is your best resource — they know the rules for every municipality they serve and many will handle the permit process on your behalf.

When You Definitely Need a Permit

  • Street placement — your dumpster is on a public road, not your property
  • Blocking a sidewalk — even partially obstructing a public sidewalk may require a permit
  • HOA communities — not a government permit, but many Florence County HOAs have their own rules about dumpsters, including placement duration and advance notification
  • Commercial properties — some commercial zoning areas have additional requirements for temporary containers

When You Don't Need a Permit

  • Driveway placement — the most common scenario; no permit needed
  • Yard or side lot — as long as you're on your own property
  • Private parking lots — with the property owner's permission
  • Construction job sites — if the dumpster is on the job site property (contractors still need building permits for the work itself)

Tips to Avoid Permit Problems

1. Measure Your Driveway First

Most permit headaches happen because the dumpster won't fit on the driveway and ends up partially in the street. Before ordering, measure your available space. A 20-yard container needs about 22 feet of length and 8 feet of width. If your driveway is too short, consider a smaller 10 or 15-yard container that fits entirely on your property.

2. Check with Your HOA

If you live in an HOA community in Florence County, check your covenants before delivery. Common HOA restrictions include maximum rental duration (often 7 days), required advance notice to the HOA board, and rules about container placement location.

3. Ask Your Provider

Local Florence County dumpster providers handle permit questions daily. When you request a quote, mention your placement situation and they'll tell you whether a permit is needed and often handle the process for you.

4. Protect Your Driveway

While not a permit issue, protecting your driveway is important. Ask your provider if they place plywood or boards under the container to prevent damage. Take photos of your driveway before delivery as documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a permit for a dumpster on my driveway in South Carolina?
No. In Florence, Darlington, Marion, and most SC cities, placing a dumpster on private property like your driveway or yard does not require a permit.
How much does a dumpster street permit cost in Florence SC?
Permit fees vary by municipality. Contact the City of Florence Public Works department at (843) 665-3236 for current fees. Many providers handle the permit process for you if street placement is needed.
Do contractors need a separate permit for construction dumpsters?
Contractors in Florence SC need building permits for construction work, but the dumpster itself doesn't require a separate permit if it's on the job site (private property). Street placement still requires a right-of-way permit.
