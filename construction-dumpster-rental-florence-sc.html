Choosing the Right Container Size for Construction Projects

Selecting the wrong size dumpster is one of the most common and costly mistakes contractors make. Here's a quick breakdown for Florence SC job sites:

Container Size Guide for Contractors

SizeBest For
10-yardSmall demo jobs, bathroom gut-outs, minor flooring removal
20-yardKitchen remodels, single-room additions, moderate framing waste
30-yardFull home renovations, roofing tear-offs, multi-room gut jobs
40-yardNew construction, large commercial demolition, major site clearing

For most residential construction projects in Florence County, a 20 or 30-yard container is the sweet spot. Commercial contractors working on larger footprints typically need 40-yard roll-off containers.

Heavy Debris: What You Need to Know

Not all dumpsters are rated for heavy construction materials. Concrete, brick, asphalt, and dirt are dense materials that can exceed weight limits on standard containers.

Heavy Debris Guidelines

  • Always ask your provider about weight limits before loading heavy materials
  • Concrete and masonry should ideally go in a dedicated heavy debris container
  • Mixed loads (wood + concrete) may incur overage fees if weight limits are exceeded
  • Most Florence SC providers charge a per-ton overage fee beyond the included weight allowance
💡 Pro Tip: Order a separate smaller container specifically for heavy materials like concrete and brick. It's often cheaper than paying overage fees on a mixed load.

Swap-Out Services for Long-Term Projects

Multi-phase construction projects in Florence SC don't have to stop when a dumpster fills up. Most local roll-off providers offer swap-out service — they haul your full container and drop a fresh empty one, usually within 24 hours.

When to Schedule a Swap-Out

  • Framing waste accumulates quickly — schedule proactively, not reactively
  • Call for a swap-out when the container is 75% full, not 100%
  • Build swap-out scheduling into your project timeline from day one

C&D Waste Compliance in Florence County

Construction and Demolition (C&D) waste disposal is regulated in South Carolina. Florence County requires that C&D materials be disposed of at licensed facilities.

Accepted C&D Materials

  • Wood, lumber, and framing materials
  • Drywall and plaster
  • Roofing shingles and underlayment
  • Metal scraps and piping
  • Concrete, brick, and block

Prohibited from C&D Dumpsters

  • Asbestos-containing materials (requires licensed abatement)
  • Lead paint debris (special handling required)
  • Chemicals, solvents, or hazardous waste
  • Tires and batteries
